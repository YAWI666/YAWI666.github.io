Vision

Professional profile

The Higher Technical Professional in Industrial Informatics has a comprehensive, investigative, creative, innovative in the technological field where it performs competitively and standards of quality and information security, respecting the environment and gender, improving the quality of life of individuals and the community as a whole

Historia

The Chamber of Senators approved granting a Camaral Declaration that pays tribute to the Institute Technological Higher Industrial School "Pedro Domingo Murillo" of the department of La Paz, in recognition of his contribution to Bolivian education, training for 77 years, professionals of excellence, with productive, research and innovation capacities to respond to the needs and socioeconomic and cultural characteristics of the Plurinational State of Bolivia.

The "Pedro Domingo Murillo" Higher Industrial School Technological Institute was created through Supreme Resolution of February 10, 1942, officially inaugurating its work on August 4 of that same year, under the direction of Professor Julio Lairana Sandoval, with ten young high school graduates who professionally trained in different specialties.
Its mission is technical and technological training with professional quality, applying knowledge scientists and innovators with an entrepreneurial spirit and job opportunities, under principles ethics, gender equity and respect for mother earth and nature, which is consistent with the vision of being the leading institution in technical and technological training in the national and international context, in constant transformation and expansion, preferred for its academic quality.
During its 77 years of institutional life, the Instituto Tecnológico Escuela Industrial Superior “Pedro Domingo Murillo”, trained professionals of excellence who contribute with their knowledge to the development of country, graduating 13,013 students at the Higher Technical level, from the first class in 1945, to the date.
It currently has a new study plan for the Basic Curriculum Design of Technical Education, with 36 races approved by Ministerial Resolution No. 082/2017. It has its own infrastructure of 64,000 m² and equipment consisting of machinery, equipment and tools obtained through donations and help from friendly countries such as Germany, Canada, Switzerland and Japan, through its cooperation agency JICA.
